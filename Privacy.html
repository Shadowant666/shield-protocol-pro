Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 22, 2026

1. Introduction

Shield Protocol Pro ("we," "our," or "the Extension") is dedicated to enhancing user security within the Web3 ecosystem. This Privacy Policy describes how we handle user data in compliance with the Chrome Web Store Developer Program Policies.

2. Information Collection and Usage

To provide its core security functionality—transaction simulation and risk reporting—the Extension processes the following data:

  • Transaction Metadata: When you initiate a transaction, we analyze the "To" address, "From" address, input data (contract calls), and transaction value. This is used solely to generate a security simulation via our processing partners (e.g., Tenderly API).
  • Account Information: We collect your PayPal email address exclusively to verify your "Pro" license status or to manage token balances associated with your account.

3. Disclosure of Data to Third Parties

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information. Data is only shared with third parties when necessary to fulfill the Extension's single purpose of transaction protection:

  • Simulation Services: Transaction details are sent securely to Tenderly to provide the "Security Insight" report before you sign a transaction.
  • Payment Processing: PayPal handles all financial transactions. We do not store or have access to your credit card or bank details.

4. Limited Use and Prohibited Practices

In accordance with Google's Limited Use policy, we certify that:

  • We only request access to user data that is necessary to provide the stated security features.
  • We do not use or transfer user data for personalized advertising, creditworthiness checks, or any form of data brokering.
  • User data is transmitted via secure, encrypted connections (HTTPS).

5. Data Retention and Security

  • Real-time Processing: Transaction data is processed in real-time and is not stored permanently on our servers.
  • Encryption: All data transmissions are protected using industry-standard TLS/SSL encryption.

6. User Consent and Rights

You may withdraw your consent at any time by uninstalling the extension. As we do not maintain persistent user profiles beyond license verification, uninstalling effectively removes all active data collection.
